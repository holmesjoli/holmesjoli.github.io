Artist Statement

While I do not come from a traditional academic arts background, I'm a very active creator. Frequently, the mediums which I am most interested in working with and exploring are those not often recognized as "high arts" such as fiber arts, ceramics, and web design. Art has always been the subject that I connected the most with. There wasn't a semester in middle school or high school where I wasn't in an art class. I started throwing on the wheel when I was 12 years old and have continue to throw since.

Research Interests

Much of my portfolio is dedicated to challenging the idea that quantitative data and technology are neutral and objective. Data are socially constructed, but due to their quantitative nature, they are frequently accepted as unbiased, neutral, and true. However, social contruction necessitates that certain values will be encoded in the data, in the collection process, and in their general use. My goal as an artist is to complicate the notion of neutrality as it applies to data and technology more generally.

Much of my interest in data stems from my work in social policy research. Administrative data is frequently used in policy research because it is already collected and managed by government agencies. The alternative way to access data for research is through primary data collection, which can be costly in time and resources. However, one large disadvantage of using administrative data is that is is not generally collected for research and may therefore be biased.

Data and research background

I graduated from Wesleyan University with a Bachelors of Arts in Economics and minor in data analytics. At Wesleyan, I was a TA for the data visualization class. Since then, I've worked as a data scientist in social policy research. I've loved working in policy research because it feels like my work positively impacts my community through data-informed decisions. Most of my programming experience is in programming languages: R and Python. As a data scientist, much of the work I do is visual in nature and it's my favorite part of the work that I do. I love being able to take a complex system or analysis and turn it into something that's more interpretable.
