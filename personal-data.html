Project

Brief

Digital platforms and mobile devices rendered us as (mostly) unconscious data producers. What happens when we start consciously collecting data about ourselves, our daily habits, or the most peculiar of our little obsessions? Starting a systematic collection of personal data and designing a visual report will help develop awareness and sensitivity around the topic. The format and the language of the report will reflect the nature of the data and its personal significance.

Data collection

Like most people, the ways in which I interact and the number of daily interactions I have has changed over significantly over the course of the last several years. For three weeks, I collected data on all of my different interactions digital and online. I collected data to answer the following three questions.

  • Who I interact with each week in person. Are they anonymous or known to me? What is the approximate number of individuals? For those known, what is their connection (e.g. school or work)?
  • Who I interact with each week digitally. What is the format (e.g. email, zoom, text)? What is their connection?
  • What time do I interact with people and for how long?
