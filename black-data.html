Black data is a term used by Andrew Ferguson in his book The Rise of Big Data Policing. WHAT IS BLACK DATA ...

Ferguson details how communities of color are policed at much higher rates than white communities and the rise in predictive policing has augmented this trend. Although sociotechnical systems may appear to be neutral and unbiased, they are often trained on data which is biased. For example, when communities of color are policed more than white communities, people of color end up in the criminal justice system at much higher rates. Predictive policing algorithms are trained on data which have been collected in the past. Since people of color and neighborhoods of color appear with greater frequency in the training data, predictive algorithms predict that crime will occur with greater frequencies in communities of color which then perpetuates the over-policing of communities of color.

The first image shows the number of arrests in for marijuana possession in Black communities compared to white communities.
